Therapy

1. Self-Assessment & Acknowledgment

Recognize the symptoms and challenges you're facing.

Accept that seeking help is a positive step toward improvement.

Understand how your thoughts, emotions, and behaviors impact your daily life.

Identify triggers that may worsen your mental health condition.

2. Identify the Right Type of Therapist

  • Psychologists – Specialize in talk therapy (CBT, DBT, etc.).
  • Psychiatrists – Can diagnose and prescribe medication.
  • Counselors/Therapists – Provide emotional support and coping strategies.
  • Clinical Social Workers – Offer therapy and guidance for social aspects.
  • Specialists – Look for experts in specific fields.

3. Find a Therapist

Use online directories, university counseling services, helplines, referrals, and online platforms.

  • Online Directories: Use platforms like Psychology Today, TherapyRoute, or local mental health portals to search for certified therapists.
  • University/College Counseling Services: Many institutions offer free or discounted therapy for students.

4. Choose the Right Therapy Approach

There are various therapy approaches tailored to different mental health needs. Understanding these can help you choose the best fit:

  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy
  • Dialectical Behavior Therapy
  • Psychodynamic Therapy
  • Humanistic Therapy

5. Schedule & Attend Sessions

Book consultations, be open in sessions, and stay consistent.

6. Consider Additional Support

Join support groups, practice self-care, and adopt healthy habits.

7. Search for Emergency Help

Seek immediate help from crisis helplines if needed.
